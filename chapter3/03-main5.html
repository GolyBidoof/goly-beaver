For the longest time, I had this glorified idea of a friend for your lifetime. And maybe I still do?

I do have a few lifetime friends, who I'm really grateful about!

Problem being, a healthy level of attachment to a person requires some convergence and divergence, and not everyone will be able to see you where you're at, at every stage of your life.
Your lifetime friend will see you struggling and will connect the dots, will identify how did your previous choices in life lead you there...

But unsurprisingly enough, your lifetime or your best friend will not make decisions for you, or live your life for you. They have their own life to take care of as well, and they will not relate to every obstacle you're going through.

Emotional validation is important. Problem solving...
